Interest in IT - A New Hope

A young farm boy, Luke, is thrust into a galaxy of adventure when he discovers an ancient tome in the great library of Trinity (Thurgoona). Silly O.T. references aside, my interest in IT began at an
early age whenI made my first webpage by following the lessons in an old "Dummy's Guide to HTML" book from the school library. I was quickly hooked on making simple pages for nearly anything - favourite books, recipes and video games were the most memorable of these.
Shortly thereafter, an uncle of mine learned of my newfound interest in creating webpages and suggested that I practice making them on an old laptop that my parents had. As was to be expected, it ran increadibly slowly. This lead to my second brush with interesting IT - linux.
My uncle showed me how to install and use a very beginner friendly disto which I still use from time to time: linux mint. Learning how the terminal worked was a lot of fun, although at this age I found it tedious, still preferring to use a UI for most things.

There were too many other experiences to list and describe here, but I feel that these were the onces that the rest were built upon. They've ultimately lead me to spend my gap year as a full time IT trainee at a local school and to my newfound interest in cybersecurity.

RMIT - Higher Education Strikes Back

The Rebels flee south, to the city of Melbourne to forge a better future for themselves. However, their income forces them into a shoebox.
I chose to come to RMIT for a number of reasons. In no particular order I'll discuss a few of these.
The campus' proximity to the CBD was a big plus in my books. I knew it would be fairly easy to access from nearly any suburb so long as I was close to a train line.
The variety of courses on offer was also an important factor for me, as I came out of my gap year without a clear vision of what I wanted to do long term. As such, starting my programme (BP083) felt like a pathway that would at least get me moving in a direction I knew would be helpful in some capacity.
The emphasis on making graduates job ready was something I was pleased to see also, as I knew I'd be keen to get back to full-time work after graduating.
